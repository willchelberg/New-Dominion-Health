# Article Formatting Guide

This guide shows you how articles will appear when readers click on them, and demonstrates all the formatting options available in your rich text editor.

## Article Page Layout

When someone clicks on an article, they'll see:

### Top Section
- **Back Button** - Returns to the blog listing
- **Article Title** - Large, centered headline in Baskerville font
- **Featured Image** - Full-width hero image with rounded corners

### Author Section
- **Author Avatar** - Circular icon with author's initial
- **Author Name** - Your name or "New Dominion Health"
- **Publication Date** - Month, Day, Year format
- **Read Time** - Automatically calculated (e.g., "5 min read")

### Social Sharing (Desktop)
- Fixed sidebar on the left with icons for:
  - Copy link
  - Twitter
  - Facebook
  - LinkedIn
  - Email

### Article Content
The main content area with beautiful typography and formatting options.

## Available Formatting Options

### Headers (H1, H2, H3)

Use headers to organize your content into sections:

**H1** - Main article title (automatically created from your post title)
**H2** - Major section headings
**H3** - Subsection headings

Example hierarchy:
```
H1: Understanding Your Body's Signals
  H2: Why Body Awareness Matters
  H2: Common Body Signals to Recognize
    H3: 1. Fatigue and Low Energy
    H3: 2. Thirst and Dry Mouth
  H2: Moving Forward
```

### Text Formatting

- **Bold** - For emphasis and important points
- *Italic* - For subtle emphasis or terms
- Underline - For highlighting
- Strikethrough - For showing changes

### Lists

**Unordered Lists** (Bullets)
- Great for non-sequential items
- Easy to scan
- Perfect for features or benefits

**Ordered Lists** (Numbers)
1. Use for step-by-step instructions
2. Sequential processes
3. Ranked items

### Blockquotes

Use blockquotes for:
- Bible verses
- Important quotes
- Key takeaways
- Expert opinions

They appear with a left border and italic styling in your brand colors.

### Links

Add hyperlinks to:
- Reference scientific studies
- Link to related articles
- Provide additional resources
- External websites

Links appear in blue-gray (#64767C) and underline on hover.

### Code Blocks

Use inline code or code blocks for:
- Sharing recipes with measurements
- Exercise routines
- Specific protocols
- Biblical references

### Alignment

Align text:
- Left (default)
- Center (for emphasis or quotes)
- Right
- Justified

### Colors

Add text or background colors for:
- Highlighting key points
- Creating visual interest
- Organizing information

## Formatting Best Practices

### 1. Start with a Strong Opening
Your first paragraph should hook the reader and explain why the topic matters.

### 2. Use Headers to Break Up Content
Don't create walls of text. Add H2 headers every 2-4 paragraphs to make content scannable.

### 3. Incorporate Lists
Lists make information easy to digest. Use them whenever you have 3+ related items.

### 4. Add Visual Emphasis
Use **bold** for important points, *italic* for emphasis, and blockquotes for key takeaways.

### 5. Include Scripture When Relevant
Blockquotes are perfect for Bible verses that support your message.

### 6. End with a Call to Action
Ask a question or suggest a next step to engage readers.

### 7. Keep Paragraphs Short
3-5 sentences per paragraph reads better on screens than long blocks of text.

## Tips for Great Articles

1. **Write conversationally** - Imagine talking to a friend
2. **Be specific** - Concrete examples are more helpful than vague advice
3. **Tell stories** - Personal experiences resonate with readers
4. **Stay on brand** - Connect health topics to faith and stewardship
5. **Edit ruthlessly** - Remove unnecessary words
6. **Add images** - Visual breaks improve readability
7. **Preview before publishing** - Use the preview toggle to see how it looks
