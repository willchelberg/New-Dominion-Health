## 🎯 Why This Typography Works

### 1. **Professional & Trustworthy**
- **Baskerville** is a classic serif font associated with:
  - Authority and credibility
  - Medical journals and academic publications
  - Traditional, established institutions
  - Professional services (law, medicine, finance)
- Perfect for healthcare where trust is paramount

### 2. **Highly Readable**
- Baskerville has excellent readability, especially for:
  - Long-form content (blog articles, medical information)
  - Screen reading
  - Older demographics (common in healthcare)
- The font was designed for printed text, making it naturally easy on the eyes

### 3. **Elegant Simplicity**
- Using ONE font for 95% of the site creates:
  - Visual consistency
  - Clean, uncluttered appearance
  - Professional polish
  - Easy maintenance

### 4. **Chapman in Navigation = Smart Hierarchy**
- Using Chapman ONLY in the header:
  - Creates clear visual separation
  - Makes navigation immediately recognizable
  - Adds modern touch without overwhelming
  - Balances traditional (Baskerville) with contemporary (Chapman)

## 🎨 Design Principles You're Following

### 1. **Font Pairing Rule: "Display + Text"**
✅ You're doing this correctly:
- **Display font** (Chapman) = navigation/wayfinding
- **Text font** (Baskerville) = content consumption

### 2. **The 80/20 Rule**
✅ You're following this:
- 80% of text in one font (Baskerville)
- 20% in accent font (Chapman)
- Prevents visual chaos

### 3. **Contrast Without Conflict**
✅ Chapman (sans-serif condensed) vs. Baskerville (serif regular)
- Different enough to create hierarchy
- Similar enough to feel cohesive
- Both are classic, professional fonts

## 💎 Specific Strengths of Your Choices

### **Baskerville URW Medium**

**Pros:**
- ✅ Designed by John Baskerville (1750s) - timeless
- ✅ High x-height = excellent web readability
- ✅ Strong stroke contrast = elegant
- ✅ Wide letter spacing = clear, open feel
- ✅ Professional without being stuffy
- ✅ Works great at all sizes (14px - 72px)

**Perfect for:**
- Article content
- Headlines
- Subheadings
- Body paragraphs
- Quotes and testimonials

### **Chapman Medium Condensed**

**Pros:**
- ✅ Condensed = space-efficient in navigation
- ✅ Modern, clean lines
- ✅ Strong presence without being loud
- ✅ Excellent for short text (navigation labels)

**Perfect for:**
- Navigation menus
- Buttons in header
- Site title
- Short wayfinding text

## 🎯 My Recommendations: KEEP YOUR CURRENT SETUP

### ✅ What You Should DO:

1. **Keep 95% Baskerville**
   - All headings (h1, h2, h3)
   - All body text
   - All buttons
   - All forms
   - Blog content
   
2. **Keep Chapman in Header Only**
   - Navigation links
   - "New Dominion Health" title
   - Mobile menu

3. **Consider Adding:**
   - **Letter-spacing** on Baskerville headings (0.01em - 0.02em)
   - **Line-height** 1.6-1.8 for body text (improves readability)

### ❌ What You Should NOT Do:

1. **Don't add a third font**
   - This would break your clean aesthetic
   - Healthcare should be simple and clear

2. **Don't use Chapman for body text**
   - Condensed fonts are harder to read in paragraphs
   - Save it for navigation only

3. **Don't switch to all sans-serif**
   - Serifs are more trustworthy for healthcare
   - Better for long-form reading
